AI Enabled CV Review

Redesigning Resume Review Experience for Great Learning Career Services

Project Overview

Context: Great Learning provides career support services as part of its Post Graduate programs. A major component is resume reviews, historically handled by external experts. These reviews were inconsistent and required internal QA.

Our Goal: Design an internal, AI-powered resume review system to improve consistency, reduce operational overhead, and support diverse learner profiles.

Problem Statement

The existing resume review process was inconsistent, manual, and unscalable. Internal teams had to audit every resume before sharing it with learners. Given the broad range of learner personas (from freshers to 20+ years of experience, across domains), the system needed heavy customization and better quality control.

User Personas & Needs

Freshers

Struggle with structure, ATS formatting, and project-based highlights.

Mid-career

Want to reframe role transitions and make long careers concise.

Senior professionals

Need leadership impact and clarity over scope

Career switchers

Seek help positioning transferable skills.

Tech roles

Need to showcase stacks, projects, GitHub, etc.

Tech roles

Require business-focused language and formatting support.

User Study Report: CV & ATS-Based Resume Review Expectations

Objective: Understand user expectations, emotions, and pain points regarding resume reviews and ATS optimization.

Participants: 15 users (5 early-career, 5 mid-career, 5 career switchers).

Methodology: Pre/post surveys, interviews, usability testing with prototype.

Key Qualitative Insights
  • Expectations: Clear, actionable feedback. Not just format-based but content-aligned with job roles.
  • Feedback Types: Keyword match, grammar, tone, ATS formatting, impact improvements.
  • Trust Factors: Explain suggestions, show examples, confidence scores.
  • Pain Points: Generic feedback, lack of context, confusing templates.
  • Emotional Drivers: Job-seekers feel anxious; they want reassurance and direction.
Usability Observations
  • Keyword suggestions caused friction due to lack of placement guidance.
  • Score alone felt meaningless without improvement cues.
  • Formatting feedback was appreciated when contextualized.
  • Technical jargon lowered engagement and understanding.

UX Strategy & Design Principles

Consistency

Replace subjective human reviews with modular, persona-driven templates.

>
Contextual Feedback

Show suggestions inline within resume preview.

Simple Copy

Use layman terms and explain feedback.

Version Control

Let users save and compare multiple resume versions.

Feature Highlights

  • Persona-based templates for freshers, mid-career, senior, switchers.
  • AI-powered bullet improvements and keyword insights.
  • Section-wise confidence heatmaps.
  • Real-time job description upload for keyword matching.
  • Admin panel for internal quality control and override.
