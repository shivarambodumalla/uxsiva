Olympus LMS Enhancement

Empowering Dreams, Accelerating Careers, and Cultivating Lifelong Learning Adventures!

Initiative to Empower Diverse Learners

Olympus LMS is undergoing a revamp to enhance the learning experience for learners pursuing PG programs, including both non-programmers and programmers, with varying work experiences ranging from 0 to 25 years. The objective is to transform the LMS into a visually appealing, structured, responsive, and intuitive platform that offers a personalized and efficient learning journey.

User Journey of Learners

User Journey of Learners

Certainly! Based on the context provided, here is a list of pain points that learners may experience with the current state of Olympus LMS, which the revamp aims to address:

1. Lack of Course Visibility
  • Difficulty in distinguishing between active, upcoming, completed, and incomplete courses.
2. Inefficient Course Information Display
  • Limited course information on course cards, making it challenging to understand course details at a glance.
  • Missing marks/grades visibility on course cards, affecting learners' ability to track their performance.
3. Navigation Issues on Course Details Page
  • Complex navigation between tabs (Content, Recordings, Discussion, Industry Focus) within the course details page.
  • Unclear completion triggers for different content types, causing confusion about course progress.
4. User Experience on Course Consumption Page
  • Difficulties in accessing course modules and bookmarks due to inefficient left navigation.
  • Lack of interactive features like bookmarks and feedback options hindering user engagement and feedback provision.
  • Unclear categorization leading to confusion about course status and progress.
5. URL Handling Challenges
  • Users encountering issues with invalid or partial URLs, leading to redirection problems and user frustration.
6. Quiz and Survey Functionality Limitations
  • Complications with quiz and survey functionalities, including pre-submission and post-submission scenarios, attempts logic, and question types.
  • Timed quizzes and different question formats causing difficulties for learners.
7. Assignment/Project Submission Concerns
  • Confusion regarding assignment/project submission types, due dates, and handling of file uploads.
  • Lack of clarity on pre-submission and post-submission processes impacting learners' assignment/project experience.
8. File Handling Issues
  • Challenges related to file types, icons, download options, and restrictions within the LMS affecting file management and access.
9. Overall Learning Journey Complexity
  • Cumbersome learning journey due to a combination of navigation, course visibility, assignment/project submission, and interactive feature challenges.
  • Lack of a structured and intuitive learning environment impacting learners' overall experience and engagement.

Addressing these pain points through the Olympus LMS revamp will significantly enhance the learning experience for learners, making it more streamlined, interactive, and user-friendly

Design and Implementation

A small team was formed comprising myself, two other developers, and a dedicated product manager for the design and implementation phase. Our approach involved collaborative brainstorming sessions to discuss user requirements, system functionalities, and technical constraints, leading to a clear roadmap.

We began by creating wireframes and prototypes to visualise the user interface and gather stakeholder feedback, refining the user experience iteratively. Tasks were divided among team members based on expertise, fostering a collaborative environment where everyone contributed to success.

During implementation, we coded, tested, and integrated components while conducting regular code reviews and testing cycles to maintain quality. Close collaboration within the team and with the product manager ensured an agile development process aligned with project goals, resulting in a successful outcome.

While addressing the primary concerns of stakeholders and Users, we have also introduced several new features. These include Excelerate, aimed at boosting career growth and job opportunities, as well as Hackathons for practicing skills and competing with peers within the batch and across organisations.

Usability testing

Since this is a crucial feature for the organization, I recruited over 50 learners to test the platform and provide their feedback before the release.

For the test, we defined 19 key tasks for the learners, and the usability test plan was structured as follows: We exposed the new platform to the learners and asked them to perform each task one by one. While they were completing the tasks, we closely monitored metrics such as task completion, time taken to complete each task, overall user experience, stress responses, questions asked, and number of mistakes made. The results were highly satisfactory, with minor hiccups that were quickly addressed. Additionally, we received subjective feedback from the learners, which we analyzed separately.

Delivery and post delivery Feedback

After receiving feedback from key stakeholders and analysing the usability test results, we gained confidence in the product's readiness. As a result, we decided to release it in a controlled manner, batch by batch. This approach was well-received by both learners and key stakeholders. The overall feedback for the product averaged around 4.78 out of 5, based on responses from approximately 1200 to 1500 learners who participated in the feedback process.
