Visa Application – For document accuracy, and real-time visibility

Objective

To streamline the international visa application journey for learners by building a centralized and intuitive feature in Olympus. The goal was to reduce learner confusion, increase document accuracy, and provide real-time visibility to the counseling team.

Context

Learners previously relied on emails, WhatsApp, and manual coordination for visa-related tasks, which caused missed deadlines and inconsistent support. The counseling team tracked progress across spreadsheets and had no consolidated learner view.

Design Solution

The experience was divided into three stages, each visualized in a step tracker:

  • Stage 1: Learners upload academic and financial documents. Status updates (Pending, Approved, Sent Back) provide immediate feedback.
  • Stage 2: Once verified, learners upload the I20 received from their university.
  • Stage 3: Visa Managers are assigned for interview prep. Learners upload visa outcomes and track approvals or rejections.

Each stage is supported by contextual nudges, help texts, and automated email updates to guide users forward.

Admin View

A dedicated JEDI dashboard was designed for counselors to:

  • View learner progress at each stage
  • Approve or reject submitted documents
  • Assign visa managers automatically after stage 2
  • Send nudges and view submission timestamps

Impact

Post-launch, visa process completion increased to over 85%, and daily support queries dropped by 75%. Learners reported greater clarity and confidence, while the counseling team gained operational efficiency and reduced turnaround time for document verification.

Key Learnings

Designing this feature taught me to balance operational workflows with learner empathy. By structuring complex processes into manageable stages and reducing cognitive load, we enabled smoother collaboration between learners and internal teams. Nudging and progressive disclosure played a major role in guiding users successfully.
