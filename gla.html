User Research: Understanding Our Learners

Our design journey began with listening. Through in-depth research across 1.6M+ learners on Great Learning Academy, we uncovered the real motivations, habits, and needs driving modern learning in India.

Who We’re Designing For

Our research spanned 1.6M+ learners across India. Here’s what we discovered about their backgrounds and motivations:

  • 62% college students and freshers seeking their first break
  • 52% complete beginners with little or no work experience
  • 72% mobile-first learners balancing study, work, and upskilling
  • Ages 18–34 the generation that learns on the move

Key Behavior Patterns

Our learners aren’t passive consumers. They’re strategic, self-driven individuals seeking growth through flexibility and focus.

  • Learning happens in micro-moments during commutes, lunch breaks, and late nights
  • Mobile for learning, laptop for practice a hybrid learning habit
  • Short-form content and visible progress maintained high motivation
  • Peer validation — seeing others succeed significantly increased engagement

What the Data Told Us

Our learner analytics revealed powerful insights that shaped the platform redesign — helping us understand where, how, and why learners engage with Great Learning Academy.

Registration Source

Web and Android showed nearly equal sign-ups, reflecting balanced usage across devices. A closer look revealed that 52% were freshers and 10% were college students — both key learner audiences.

Device Usage

72% learners accessed via mobile compared to 28% on desktop. This insight drove a critical shift toward a mobile-first experience — optimizing navigation, readability, and feature accessibility for smaller screens.

Age Distribution

The majority of learners fell within the 18–34 age range, underscoring the importance of fast, flexible, and career-relevant learning experiences tailored to young professionals and students seeking real-world growth.

Educational Background

Learners came from a diverse mix of academic disciplines — 16% Computer Science, 3.7% Electronics & Communication, and 3.3% Business Studies. This blend highlighted the platform’s role as a bridge between tech and non-tech career transitions.

Bridging Learning to Career Transitions

As Great Learning Academy empowered millions of learners to start their upskilling journey, the next evolution was clear — creating a bridge for professionals ready to accelerate their careers. Our research uncovered a growing segment seeking faster, outcome-oriented learning that directly impacted employability and growth.

The Pro Opportunity

Deeper analysis revealed a powerful insight about mid-level professionals aged 25–35 — they wanted learning experiences that directly led to tangible career outcomes. This audience wasn’t just motivated by curiosity, but by measurable growth.

  • 78% expressed willingness to subscribe for faster, career-focused learning.
  • Career-oriented features saw 3× higher engagement compared to general learning modules.
  • The biggest friction point was time-to-skill acquisition — professionals wanted speed with structure.

Insight: Professionals don’t just want to learn — they want AI-powered career acceleration.

The Insight: The “Aha!” Moment

User research revealed a pivotal behavioral pattern that redefined our design philosophy: learners preferred studying on mobile but practicing on laptops. This dual-device habit reflected a generation that valued flexibility without compromising depth.

Recognizing this, we designed Great Learning Academy Pro — an AI-powered, subscription-based platform built for professionals aged 25–35, combining personalized guidance, adaptive learning paths, and measurable skill outcomes.

The result was a career acceleration ecosystem that merged flexibility with structure — helping learners upskill faster, smarter, and with clearer professional direction.

Design Philosophy: Mobile-First, Career-Focused

When redesigning Great Learning Academy for mobile, we didn’t just scale down the desktop experience — we reimagined learning from the ground up. Our goal was to create a platform that respected our learners’ time, environment, and ambitions.

Core Design Foundations

1. Micro-Learning Architecture

We built the experience around 5–10 minute learning moments, enabling users to learn in short bursts — during commutes, breaks, or downtime — without losing continuity. This turned learning into a flexible, daily ritual.

2. Career-Outcome Mapping

Every design decision reinforced a single truth — learning must lead to measurable career growth. Course flows, progress tracking, and recommendations were directly tied to industry-relevant skills and outcomes.

3. Seamless Device Ecosystem

Learners could start on mobile, practice on desktop, and switch effortlessly between devices. This hybrid continuity supported real-world learning habits and improved completion rates.

The Challenge of Mobile Learning

Designing for mobile education introduced unique challenges traditional e-learning rarely faced. Each was met through iterative UX strategy, data-backed decisions, and continuous testing with real learners.

Attention Fragmentation

With constant interruptions — calls, notifications, movement — our design emphasized graceful recovery, enabling smooth pause–resume learning without friction.

Context Switching

Learners often jumped between apps. We prioritized continuity, ensuring progress was auto-saved and accessible instantly across sessions and devices.

Limited Screen Real Estate

Complex topics were distilled into bite-sized visuals and micro-interactions — preserving depth while avoiding overload.

Varied Network Conditions

With learners across India’s diverse connectivity spectrum, we implemented offline-first principles and progressive content loading for uninterrupted access.

Touch-First Interactions

Every gesture, button, and navigation element was optimized for thumb-friendly, one-handed use, supporting genuine on-the-go learning.

Our Design Principles

Our core design system revolved around human-centered simplicity and emotional connection. We translated psychological insights and user behavior into a modular, rewarding, and deeply personal experience.

  • 1. Respect Cognitive Load

    A clean visual hierarchy and progressive disclosure helped users stay focused in high-distraction environments.

  • 2. Celebrate Small Wins

    Micro-rewards like streaks, badges, and milestone pop-ups encouraged consistency and built learner confidence.

  • 3. Visual Learning First

    Abstract or technical concepts were transformed into visual storytelling and real-world analogies for quick comprehension.

  • 4. Career Context Always

    Skill tags and outcome dashboards continuously reinforced relevance to learners’ professional goals.

  • 5. Community-Driven Motivation

    Peer validation and achievement sharing turned solitary study into social growth.

The mobile learning interface prioritized readability, progress visibility, and motivation — built to help learners grow anytime, anywhere.

Visual Design: Where Function Meets Emotion

Designing for Great Learning Academy meant striking a balance between trust and motivation — creating an interface that feels professional yet human, structured yet inspiring. The visual system evolved to embody both the rigor of career-focused learning and the warmth of personal growth.

Design System Evolution

To ensure visual consistency across the platform, we developed a scalable design system grounded in accessibility, clarity, and emotional resonance.

  • Color Palette: Guided by psychology to drive emotion and engagement
  • Typography: Legible and hierarchical, optimized for mobile readability
  • Iconography: Minimal and functional, reinforcing intuitive navigation
  • Component Library: Modular elements ensuring design consistency and rapid iteration

Design Language

Our visual language was guided by a simple philosophy: “Empower learners through design that feels achievable, not intimidating.” It had to balance credibility for professionals with friendliness for beginners.

Color Psychology

  • Primary Blue: Evokes trust, focus, and stability
  • Success Green: Symbolizes growth, progress, and achievement
  • Warning Orange: Used sparingly for motivational alerts
  • Neutral Grays: Maintain calm, distraction-free focus

Typography Hierarchy

  • Primary Typeface: Modern sans-serif with professional clarity
  • Hierarchy: Structured rhythm for headers and body text
  • Readability: Tuned for small screens and consistent contrast
  • Microcopy: Supportive and human, designed to reassure learners
